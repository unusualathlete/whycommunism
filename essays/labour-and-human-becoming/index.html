labour and human becoming

how this was written

this started as discord messages to a friend while i was trying to understand what was going on with the color blue. ive organized it into an essay and added evidence afterward. but most of what u are about to read is word for word from those DMs. including the HOLY SHITs.

blue

wine-dark → blue: a colour being laboured into the world

ive been thinking about labour and human becoming.

many ancient languages did not isolate blue in the way modern english does. blue was often one of the last basic color words to become distinct. and they literally described the sky and water as iron or dark wine in Homer.

and obviously im not saying their eyeballs physically couldnt receive blue light. im saying blue was not yet as separated, fulfilled, and meaningful as a human quality as it is now. humanity doesnt just open its eyes and receive a completed world with every difference already separated and named. we develop those differences by labouring with the world. blue more and more became something we made through pigments and dyes, then through paint, glass, fabrics, printing, screens, etc.

and NO, communication, attention, and technology arent separate explanations from labour. communication about WHAT? attention toward WHAT? why would people pay attention to and communicate about a difference everyday unless it became important through labour?

technology is literally accumulated labour and knowledge made into tools. technology is previous labour standing beside current labour and allowing it to begin from a higher level.

so communication is labour learning how to talk about what it has made. attention is labour training the senses where to look. technology is past labour coming back as a tool.

and this made me think about why we have such limited descriptions for taste or smell. i think its because most people barely labour with those things as active senses. for taste and smell, we usually describe things through objects we already produce or use. “it tastes like cloth.” “it smells like wood.” “it tastes metallic.” instead of having a name for the actual quality, we just say it is like another thing.

but when a person actively uses a sense in production, an entire world opens up. chefs and coffee growers can notice differences that most people barely even know exist. so can perfumers, mechanics, and people who make tea or wine. the sensory organ has to be actively involved in the labour process. the person works on the object, but the object also trains the person.

the hand shapes the object, but the object trains the hand.

the ear listens to music, but music develops the ear.

the tongue tastes food, but cooking develops the tongue.

labour doesnt only create an object. it creates the human power that can properly experience that object.

the water problem

a taste society has not yet built words for

oh! and people barely have any words to describe the taste of water!

for me personally i am kinda like a water connoisseur. i am a snob to distilled or purified water because it tastes like medicine, while fresh water literally from a lake, evian spring water, spring tap water, and different bottled waters can taste very delicious and completely different to me.

but i dont have any description for it other than “yummy,” “fresh,” “clean,” “soft,” or maybe “heavy.” my tongue experiences differences that society has not developed enough language for.

and i mean society at large here. foodies already have huge technical vocabularies. perfumers, tea people, wine people, all of them do too. thats exactly the point. when a sense becomes actively involved in a developed labour process, the distinctions and language EXPLODE inside that community. this just hasnt happened with the taste of water across society because most people dont actively labour with water according to taste.

we gather it from nature, clean it, filter it, bottle it, transport it, make it safe. the labour isnt mainly related to developing its taste.

so i think in a different world, when humanity becomes more creative and the productive forces are socialised inside a true human community, we could have an explosion like no other in descriptions of taste and smell.

everyone could actually become like me today with water! everyone could become a water connoisseur!!

people could pick between waters from this or that special lake, this spring or that spring. there could be completely synthetic taste-designed waters with different minerals and textures, maybe even different temperatures or smells. children might learn words for water the way we learn words for colors.

humanity would not only make more kinds of water. humanity would create a richer tongue and a more developed sense of taste.

and theres actual sensory evidence for this!!

professional perfumers were able to mentally create smells faster and more successfully than student perfumers. years of practice were associated with functional reorganization in the olfactory and memory areas of the brain.

so the labour doesnt just give them more words. it develops the actual power to internally reproduce and manipulate the smell even when the object isnt physically there. the nose becomes an olfactory imagination 😭

show me the evidence
yes, the nose really does change

professional perfumers and perfumery students were asked to imagine odors from chemical names. both groups activated areas involved in smelling even though no odor was physically present, and the pattern changed with experience. another study found structural differences in olfactory brain regions among perfumers, with some of those differences related to years of professional practice.

so the “olfactory imagination” part isnt just me being poetic. this is what a trained olfactory imagination can actually look like.

but im being careful here: these were small studies comparing people who had already become perfumers. because the researchers met them after they entered the field, the studies cannot completely separate what training changed from who was drawn into perfumery in the first place.

either process would still be material and physically present in the brain. the limitation is about which process caused how much of the difference, not a separation between thought and matter.

and the ear has evidence too. a 2022 review looked at 62 longitudinal studies involving 3,928 people and found small average improvements in auditory and linguistic processing after music training. small is still real. “music develops the ear” does not mean music gives everybody unlimited intelligence. it means sustained musical labour can develop some of the powers actually used in listening.

  1. Jane Plailly, Chantal Delon-Martin, and Jean-Pierre Royet, “Experience Induces Functional Reorganization in Brain Regions Involved in Odor Imagery in Perfumers” (2012)
  2. Chantal Delon-Martin et al., “Perfumers’ Expertise Induces Structural Reorganization in Olfactory Brain Regions” (2013)
  3. Leonor Neves et al., “Does Music Training Enhance Auditory and Linguistic Processing?” (2022)

AND theres an almost perfect study for what im saying about color. “Color Naming Across Languages Reflects Color Use” found that the Tsimane’ were much more likely to use color words for artificial objects than natural objects. the researchers argue that industrialization makes color more useful because it creates stable manufactured objects that can be identical except for hue.

also, blue and green were communicated less efficiently because they occur so much as backgrounds like sky, water, and vegetation. warm colors more often identify discrete objects.

so production creates the practical need for the distinction. language stabilizes it and passes it on. then the developed language can feed back into attention.

show me the evidence
color becomes clearer when people need to use it

the 2017 study “Color Naming Across Languages Reflects Color Use” looked at color communication across languages and also ran a small experiment with Tsimane’ speakers. in that experiment, people were more likely to use a color word when distinguishing artificial objects than natural ones.

the same paper looked at thousands of images and found that the objects standing out from a scene tended to be warm-colored, while blue and green appeared more often in backgrounds. that gives a practical reason why warm colors could become easier to communicate while blue stayed less useful as a property for identifying separate objects.

this is extremely close to what i was trying to say, but i dont want to make the study prove more than it does. only 28 Tsimane’ participants took part in the artificial-object task, and it did not watch a language develop across history. a later reanalysis also argued that perception itself explains part of the warm/cool difference, although communicative need still mattered.

and homer is the image that got me thinking. it is not an eye test. “dark wine” cannot prove what an ancient greek person could physically see.

so nature is still there. but practical use is still helping decide which differences become socially urgent.

  1. Edward Gibson et al., “Color Naming Across Languages Reflects Color Use” (2017)
  2. Noga Zaslavsky et al., “Color Naming Reflects Both Perceptual Structure and Communicative Need” (2019)

and yeah, im not trying to defend the strong version of sapir-whorf where language determines what a person is capable of thinking or perceiving. language isnt some independent first cause. language grows around life-activity. labour and practical need develop distinctions, language stabilizes and transmits them, and then the language can make those distinctions easier to notice and communicate.

so i wonder if people back then genuinely didnt see blue in the fulfilled way we do now, similar to how our taste of water is basically nonexistent as a developed social sense today but could become much more complete later.

labour doesnt simply produce objects that satisfy desires which were already sitting inside humans from the beginning.

labour produces the objects, meanings, senses, and categories through which desire itself takes form.

the body gives us capacities and possibilities. it doesnt give us a finished list of what is beautiful, attractive, delicious, important, or worth wanting.

labour creates the world in which those possibilities become actual human desires.

HOLD ON. people had to make blue stay still in the world before blue could stay still in the eye.

history disappears

one body given the world’s labour; one body worn down by it

and so like im thinking about one major reason white skin, blonde hair, and blue eyes have been made so desirable across societies shaped by european empire.

and not being racist, but i think a major part of it is that these traits became attached to the people with the MOST claim to society’s LABOUR by living in or being associated with the imperial core. whiteness became connected with the populations who had the largest command over accumulated labour and everything it produced.

and this claim to society’s labour isnt some abstract thing. class makes u live it every day.

if u are proletarian or an employee, labour appears as something u have to give away in order to live. somebody else sets the purpose and pace of your activity, then claims the result. even your own human powers can begin to feel like something u rent out by the hour.

for the bourgeoisie, the owners of capital, labour appears as a power they can direct. an intention inside their head can return as a real change in the world because other people’s activity carries it out. command over social labour can begin to feel like personal capability.

live inside either relation every day and of course it enters how u think. one class experiences its own labour returning as somebody else’s power. the other experiences other people’s labour returning as its own.

so when i say one group has a greater claim to society’s labour, i dont only mean they possess more things. i mean their will is amplified by the labour of society. they say “make this” and the world moves.

under capitalism, the people making the world do not have the greatest claim to the world they made.

and then empire gives that labour relation a body.

under european empire, whiteness was shown as the owner, ruler, judge, and consumer. the products of humanity’s labour gathered around it.

blackness was violently placed at almost the exact opposite pole through slavery, colonialism, stolen land, and forced labour. asian and brown people were placed in different positions depending on empire, class, and their position in the international division of labour.

and im not saying every white person is bourgeoisie or owns capital. im saying whiteness became connected with a population that was treated as having a greater claim to the accumulated labour of humanity. even a poor white worker could still be treated as relatively closer to the imperial centre. as somebody more respectable. somebody who naturally belonged in wealthy and powerful spaces.

and “whiteness” isnt only skin. it can appear through your accent, clothes, confidence, or simply how close you seem to upperclass or european life. blonde hair and blue eyes become intensified symbols because they are especially linked with the northern european image at the centre of imperial power.

whiteness being the absence of racialization fits here too. the white person gets to appear as simply a universal individual instead of being constantly reduced to a racialized position in the division of labour. when italians or scottish people became white, they were admitted into that unmarked position. they didnt acquire a new physical feature.

there is also an older labour meaning underneath this. lighter skin could mean that somebody didnt have to labour outside under the sun. darker skin could mean agricultural labour. soft hands could mean other hands worked for u. clean clothing and straight teeth could show the same thing. so could smooth skin and the appearance of rest. society’s labour gathered around your body instead of destroying it.

and this doesnt mean european empire invented every lighter-skin hierarchy from nothing. local class societies could already connect lighter skin with protection from outdoor labour. empire entered those histories, reorganized them, and then projected whiteness as if it were a universal human standard.

show me the evidence
but the preference has labour and empire inside it

a 2026 systematic review brought together 43 studies on cosmetic skin whitening. people did not describe it only as wanting to look prettier. some were thinking about how employers saw them. others connected it to marriage or status. colorism and advertising pressed on all of it.

most of those studies only captured people at one point in time. they show the pressures people report, not that lighter skin automatically wins somebody a job or a partner. and in one résumé experiment in India, the lighter-skinned candidates did not get a significant advantage.

but the review still matters because people themselves repeatedly experience skin color as connected to material access and social acceptance. the “preference” is not floating alone inside the eye. it is attached to the life people think a body will be allowed to enter.

  1. Simon N. Williams et al., “Why Are People Cosmetic Skin Whitening? A Systematic Review” (2026)
  2. Ramya M. Vijaya and Naureen Bhullar, “Colorism and Employment Bias in India” (2022)

so under class society, one major thing beauty is made to say is that this body has been protected and given access to the world’s labour.

and ugliness is often forced to say this body has been overworked or used up.

then society blames the damaged body for carrying the marks of what society did to it.

media is related, but i think media saturation is less fundamental. media is a labour process too. making movies and advertisements actively places certain people at the centre and gives them the hero role, romance, close-ups, and full humanity.

other bodies become the servant. the threat. the joke. the background character. the person who dies first.

but media is not the beginning of this. society first gives one population a larger claim on the human world, then makes the visible marks of that claim appear beautiful.

then media concentrates those meanings, spreads them everywhere, and makes them feel natural.

history disappears and domination whispers “its just your preference.”

but the preference has labour and empire inside it.

abs and fit people

human physical power becoming visible

and this literally goes into why abs and fit people can be more attractive. they appear to have a broader range of labour capabilities in the physical realm than somebody who is less physically developed.

a fit body appears more capable of acting on the world. the attraction isnt simply toward a random shape. it is toward human physical power becoming visible.

but even that body doesnt only contain the person’s own exercise. it can also contain access to a life where society’s labour protected and developed the body instead of wearing it down. time to exercise. good food. rest. a body that wasnt destroyed at work.

and theres a contradiction here.

society loves labour when it appears as capability in a fit body, but hates labour when it appears as rough hands, disability, or old age.

the muscular athlete is praised as disciplined. the manual worker whose body was consumed by labour can be treated as ugly or lazy.

disabled people and elderly people are often treated as less attractive or even as people who should not have sexuality at all because capitalism measures human worth by whether a person’s labour can produce profit. once society decides someone cannot labour in the approved way, it starts acting as if they cannot desire or be desired either.

love doesnt naturally follow a workplace productivity chart. society has just been trained to confuse human value with labour capability and productivity under capital.

but the ideal isnt always “more labour capability = more attractive.” the plump woman example makes that obvious. a body can be attractive because it appears capable of labour, OR because it appears protected from compulsory labour and surrounded by abundance.

OH WAIT yeah and that connects directly to the marriage and household part.

patriarchal beauty divides human powers by gender. the man is made attractive as the body that can work and command. the woman can be made attractive as the body that is fed, fertile, and less able to leave.

so plumpness can mean abundance and freedom from compulsory productive labour. but at the same time it can make her more confined to reproductive labour and more controllable inside the household.

nature enters too. fertility is not made out of nothing by labour. the full theory is nature AND labour, not labour magically acting alone.

the world we did not make

the commodity disappears. the carbon stays.

WAIT. nature enters much more fully than fertility.

think about a sunset filling the whole sky, or flowers opening beside a green meadow. think about light moving across a river and then becoming completely still in a lake.

we did not labour the sun into setting. a river did not wait for a human word before it began moving. nature has powers of its own. it gives us beauty that no person put there.

but we dont meet that beauty with an empty eye either. the same human being who learned to separate blue through pigments can carry that developed eye back outside and find more inside the sky. labour did not make the sky blue. it helped make a person capable of finding so much inside that blue.

so the beauty is not only inside nature or only inside the human eye. it happens in the meeting between a real nature and a human being nature helped make, whose senses history kept developing.

nature is not just material waiting for us to give it form. it forms us too.

and this is where capitalism becomes horrifying in a completely different way.

capital is our accumulated labour returning as a power over us. capitalism organizes that power around one command. it has to come back as more capital.

so a forest standing alive can look economically idle. cut down, it moves through the market. a clean river can appear to be doing nothing while the factory poisoning it appears productive. a green meadow becomes “unused land” because nothing is accumulating there except life.

climate change is what happens when that logic becomes planetary. centuries of human knowledge and labour become a productive power enormous enough to alter the atmosphere, but the people whose labour keeps it moving do not collectively decide what that power is for.

the commodity disappears after somebody uses it. the carbon stays.

the atmosphere remembers the labour process after the market has forgotten it.

capital counts the commodity as wealth and leaves the changed climate outside the calculation. but nature keeps the account anyway.

THATS SO INSANE. we developed eyes capable of loving the world while our accumulated labour was being organized into a system capable of destroying what those eyes learned to love.

the world we make makes us. and the nature we destroy returns to remake us too.

not because nature is punishing us. because nature is real. it does not care that the destruction was profitable.

so taking our labour back also means taking responsibility for what our labour does to the world that formed us. nature would not be scenery outside communism. it would be one of the relationships communism finally makes conscious.

show me the evidence
yes, the atmosphere remembers the labour process

the IPCC concluded that human activities, mainly through greenhouse-gas emissions, unequivocally caused global warming. for 2011–2020, global surface temperature was about 1.1°C above 1850–1900.

and the responsibility is not some equal fog called “humanity.” the same IPCC statement says the historical and ongoing contributions are unequal. it connects them to how energy is used, how land is transformed, and how production and consumption are organized.

so the physical movement is established. production releases gases that remain in the atmosphere and change the climate long after the individual commodity has been used up.

the IPCC does not call that alienated labour or prove the whole marxist explanation for me. climate science establishes what the production system is doing to nature. the argument that accumulated labour has taken the form of capital, and therefore moves according to accumulation instead of conscious human need, is the historical explanation im bringing to that evidence.

    labour + nature + the individual

    nature gives possibilities · labour builds the world · each life makes its own sentences

    i was thinking of extending the labour + nature + individual experience towards the labour around them too. especially gender and sexuality.

    NATURE gives us bodies and possibilities, but it doesnt give us a finished gender identity or sexuality waiting to simply unfold on its own.

    LABOUR and social reproduction build the human world where masculinity and femininity become lived realities. where desire gains its meaning. where sexuality becomes something deeply human rather than merely biological. where family takes one form instead of another. where certain ways of loving or being are recognised while others are ignored or condemned.

    and every INDIVIDUAL moves through that world differently, taking some parts into themselves, rejecting others, reshaping others, until they gradually become the particular person they are.

    this explanation already exists in psychology. the nature vs nurture debate. or genetics and the environment.

    but what this is specifying and expanding is that nurture and environment are not static backgrounds. they are themselves produced and reproduced through labour.

    nurture and environment are historically organized through LABOUR.

    and this finally extends marxism into categories that most people today think only belong to economics and history. it extends and systematizes ideas already present in marx into a general framework that can be applied much more broadly than marx himself explicitly developed.

    childhood

    the labour of developing the future person
    lab

    and this also means childhood isnt just a timeless biological box that stays socially identical forever. obviously children are physically and mentally developing, but society decides what childhood actually looks like in everyday life through labour.

    school, family, technology, and the way society prepares the next generation all shape what “being a child” means.

    in modern capitalism, school becomes the main labour process for most children. studying doesnt usually produce a finished commodity or a wage immediately, but it produces abilities, knowledge, and future labour power.

    a child can spend most of their waking life studying, completing assignments, developing skills, and preparing to enter adult production.

    childhood is therefore not simply the absence of labour. it is organised around a particular kind of labour. the labour of developing the future person.

    but people experience this world individually. the same labour process does not enter everybody’s mind in the same way.

    two children can sit inside the exact same classroom. one child might experience studying as becoming more capable, more loved, and more able to shape the world.

    another child might experience the exact same studying as something that slowly empties them instead.

    so the fact that people develop different personalities and desires does not disprove the labour basis.

    the mode of production creates the general field of possible meanings through LABOUR. each person’s individual life then lives those meanings differently.

    the shared labour process creates the language of possible meanings. each individual life makes its own sentences.

    NO, dont replace labour with a softer and vaguer word like “activity.” labour is the entire point.

    activity could mean literally anything. LABOUR explains why the activity becomes socially necessary, repeatedly organised, taught, measured, rewarded, punished, and connected to the rest of human life.

    mental illness

    what time feels like. what authority feels like.

    and for mental illness, labour can move in almost the opposite direction from desire.

    free and developing labour expands the senses, knowledge, confidence, and personality. alienated and degrading labour works against the person and makes their own activity feel hostile.

    school is often the first labour process controlling nearly the entire waking day. wake up. arrive on time. sit still. finish the task. receive the grade. prepare for the next day. repeat.

    school doesnt only put facts inside the mind. it teaches what time feels like. what authority feels like. whether your labour belongs to u. whether your labour changes the world or only earns permission to continue.

    anxiety can grow when labour is lived under permanent judgement and expected failure.

    depression can grow when activity no longer comes back as power. when effort feels useless, commanded from outside, and unable to meaningfully change the world.

    show me the evidence
    whether your labour belongs to u

    the World Health Organization treats work as a social determinant of mental health. decent work can give people confidence and a sense of accomplishment. work can also become dangerous when people are overloaded and have no control. so can insecurity or an authoritarian boss. bullying and discrimination enter the person too.

    that is almost exactly the split i am trying to describe: labour can return to the person as developed power, or return as something hostile.

    school pressure has its own evidence. a 2023 systematic review examined 52 studies, and 48 found an association between academic pressure or the timing of the school year and at least one mental-health outcome.

    most of those studies only looked at one moment, so they cannot give me one simple cause for every child. they support the pattern while leaving the individual differences completely real.

    1. World Health Organization, “Guidelines on Mental Health at Work” (2022)
    2. Thomas Steare et al., “The Association Between Academic Pressure and Adolescent Mental Health Problems: A Systematic Review” (2023)

    the same world-forming power that can build a musician’s ear can also build a person who trembles whenever they are evaluated.

    the cage and gravity

    every drop in the study already lives under gravity

    capitalism is basically the default everywhere. it is the background condition inside which modern people are born, raised, taught, employed, entertained, judged, and studied.

    trying to completely prove capitalism’s effect while every person in the study already lives under capitalism is like trying to study the behaviour of water without gravity BEFORE humanity reached space or gained any ability to change gravitational conditions.

    gravity is inside every drop of water. if gravity is everywhere in the experiment, u can compare different kinds of water under gravity, but u cannot simply switch gravity off and see what water becomes outside it.

    capitalism is similarly inside the institutions and everyday life of the people being studied.

    so every comparison will still have history inside it. none of them will be laboratory-pure. we can still watch what changes across history and what happens when people gain more control over their labour. none of those comparisons switches capitalism off completely, but they can still show us what the labour relation is doing.

    thats a real limit on what the evidence can prove.

    psychology or biology can still discover a completely real immediate mechanism. but that mechanism can also have been formed inside a deeper historical world.

    research might explain HOW a certain fear or desire operates inside the person. it doesnt automatically prove that the mechanism developed completely outside history and labour.

    a cage does not become the natural shape of a bird just because every bird in the experiment was born inside a cage.

    OH MY GOD… MEAT

    concentrated accumulated labour that a person can eat

    OH MY GOD… MEAT IS THE MOST LABOUR INTENSIVE FOOD. THATS WHY ITS THE MOST DESIRED!!! HOLYYY

    okay no, i dont mean every meat takes more work than every other food. im seeing it as concentrated labour that people can literally eat.

    and there is no built-in finished human nature telling everybody from the beginning that meat is automatically the best food.

    before people developed animal farming, societies built their diets around whatever labour they had already developed, like farming grain or fishing.

    once animal farming developed, meat gathered the whole labour process of raising animals, from the land and feed all the way to slaughter and preservation. it became concentrated accumulated labour that a person could eat.

    and if it was mainly consumed by elites, that doesnt counter the theory AT ALL. it makes the theory stronger. elites monopolised the food with a massive concentration of social labour inside it, so meat became a sign of power and abundance.

    greater embodied labour created scarcity and restricted access. restricted access connected it with power. power increased its prestige and desirability.

    show me the evidence
    meat really does move with material abundance

    across a dataset covering 72 countries, income explained much more of the difference in terrestrial-meat consumption than it did for fish. meat consumption rose with income and then began to level off at high incomes. that does not prove labour intensity created the desire, but it does show how closely access to meat tracks a society’s command over material abundance.

    and the feast part is not a random metaphor. a major review of anthropological and archaeological research found wide agreement that feasts have repeatedly helped create prestige and political power. they have also been used to mobilize work and make inequality visible.

    so i have evidence for the movement im seeing. meat moves with greater material command. feasts have repeatedly turned special food into a display of surplus.

    what no study can hand me by itself is the last step: whether people are learning to desire the human power they feel inside it. thats my theory. im not hiding it inside the citation.

    1. Rosamond L. Naylor et al., “Blue Food Demand Across Geographic and Temporal Scales” (2021)
    2. Brian Hayden and Suzanne Villeneuve, “A Century of Feasting Studies” (2011)

    pacman did not become worse. tetris did not rot. society’s productive powers expanded around them.

    so “retro” isnt something secretly living inside pacman. retro is the relationship between an unchanged product and a society whose total accumulated labour has moved beyond it.

    a product’s desirability depends not only on its own fixed qualities, but on the amount and range of developed social labour inside it relative to its historical time.

    WAIT. CHESS.

    thats why chess endures after all these years.

    every game begins from the exact same position. and then two opponents labour against each other’s minds until they build an entire little world of warfare that did not exist before that game.

    every move changes what the other person can see. u make a threat and they answer it. they create something and now u have to understand it or be destroyed by it. their thinking comes back to u as enjoyment. and if u keep playing, it literally deepens your own skill.

    a single tournament game can hold two hours of your life, sometimes much more. even a friendly match can keep two people across from each other for hours, building one position together move by move.

    each player is developing the other player while trying to defeat them.

    a chess computer is obviously an incredible feat of human ingenuity. but playing one usually doesnt feel as alive as playing another person. it can give u stronger moves, but it cannot give u the same feeling of another person sitting there, building the position WITH U and learning u while u learn them.

    and when its in person with a good friend or a chess teammate training with u, theres something so sweet about it. u are creating a tiny war together, but the war is also care. each person gives the other a real difficulty to grow through. u leave the game with more of the other person’s mind inside your own.

    the strongest games do not merely contain the developers’ labour. they create structures where the player’s own living labour can enter, accumulate, become visible, and transform the experience.

    this also explains why a game with an enormous budget can still feel dead. thousands of hours making decorative assets can remain external to the player.

    labour has to become something the player can actually experience as human power.

    so the real principle isnt simply “people like objects with lots of labour.”

    human beings desire objects where they can experience and further develop human labour.

    the final communist argument will not be a paragraph

    OKAY AND PEOPLE LIKE CAPITALISM SO MUCH BECAUSE IT IS THE MOST LABOUR-EXPLOSIVE PHENOMENON IN HISTORY!!!

    capitalism created the biggest explosion of productive labour humanity has ever experienced. it filled everyday life with human powers that previous generations could barely imagine.

    people do not only hear arguments for capitalism. they experience its productive powers through all five senses everyday.

    capitalism does not merely defend itself with a paragraph. it arrives as a world that feels faster, richer, easier, and more powerful than anything that came before.

    and the “socialist” experiments of the past were behind that labour production. they usually began in poor, agricultural, war-destroyed, or isolated countries whose productive forces were already behind the imperial capitalist core.

    so even when they called themselves socialist, a huge part of their real historical task was still catching up. they had to develop the productive forces before they could move beyond them.

    people compared shortage with abundance. older technology with newer technology. sacrifice with visible comfort.

    calling something socialism did not automatically make its labour process more advanced. productive forces do not obey political labels.

    so to make people like communism, u have to REALLY SHOW IT TO THEIR FIVE SENSES that labour under socialism and communism can become freer, richer, more creative, and more fulfilled than it is now.

    so far the “proof” has mostly been theoretical. capitalism exploits. capitalism destroys. capitalism alienates. all true, but these only explain why the current world is cruel. they do not let somebody taste the next world.

    communism has to mean a richer human life. better food. more interesting water. more beautiful places. deeper relationships. more creative games. more fully developed people.

    not less pleasure defended by a lecture. not grey sameness called equality. not telling ordinary people to consume less while a small group still decides everything.

    socialising the productive forces should not flatten difference. it should create an explosion of qualitative difference beyond what profit can tolerate.

    THERE HAS TO BE A REAL MOVEMENT AND A SUCCESSFUL FIRST.

    capitalism did not defeat the old world because merchants wrote the most correct argument. it developed labour processes the old society could no longer contain.

    communism also has to become something people can enter and experience before it becomes the whole world. places that genuinely work better. food that is actually better. technology people can shape themselves. education that makes the mind feel larger. planning that listens instead of only commanding. public spaces so beautiful that private luxury looks lonely.

    the struggle for communism is also the struggle to make associated labour sensuously irresistible.

    the final communist argument will not be a paragraph.

    it will be somebody walking into a new social relation and saying...
