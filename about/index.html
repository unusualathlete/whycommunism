What to expect

  • Study together. Find reading material, ask questions, compare interpretations, and take part in sustained discussions that do not assume everyone already knows the vocabulary.
  • Think internationally. Meet comrades across countries, backgrounds, and experiences. No country can finish the struggle alone, and no movement can understand the world from one location.
  • A principled, moderated space. Agreement on every question is not the price of entry. Good faith, respect, and a willingness to learn are. The community is moderated against harassment, bigotry, and abuse.
  • Join theory to praxis. The point is not to collect correct opinions. It is to develop the understanding, coordination, discipline, feedback, and collective confidence that serious political work requires.

Our principles

  • United Marxist Pact works toward a true human community and the emancipation of the proletariat.
  • It stands for principled internationalism, production for need, ecological repair, and forms of organization capable of learning, correcting themselves, and holding to a clear programme.
  • Within the movement, it opposes the forces that weaken proletarian independence or reproduce domination: class collaboration, social chauvinism, patriarchy, sectarianism, dogmatism, opportunism, revisionism, populism, social democracy, and reformism.
  • Theory must become praxis, and praxis must remain open to criticism, feedback, and correction.
