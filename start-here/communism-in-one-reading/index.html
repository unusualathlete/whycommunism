Communism in One Reading

A revolution does not bake bread.

It can break the power that kept a field, factory, or warehouse in private hands. It can defeat a government and open every locked office. Then morning comes. Water still has to run clean. A premature baby still needs warmth through the night. Grain must reach the mill before people reach the end of yesterday’s loaf.

This is where communism becomes more than a flag or a promise.

Suppose the working class has overthrown capitalist rule. Who keeps the electrical grid balanced? Who decides which buildings become homes? How does a clinic report that it is running out of insulin? What happens when a former owner tries to reclaim a factory by force? How do people prevent the new coordinators from hardening into new rulers? Which forests must be left alone, and which villages need roads?

Every part of the communist programme lives inside these questions.

The map begins with one destination: a classless society where associated producers govern their common life, production answers directly to human and ecological need, and no separate state power rules over society. The road begins inside capitalism, under conditions people did not choose. It has to be made by people who are changing those conditions and themselves at the same time.

First, look at what must live

Before a society can discuss its ideals, bodies make demands.

People need food, water, shelter, medicine, care, energy, sanitation, knowledge, affection, and rest. Tools wear down. Children have to learn. Soil needs time to recover. A storm can tear through plans written the night before.

Materialism starts here. It studies people as living beings within nature who produce their lives through social activity. Ideas matter enormously, since an idea can organize a strike, justify a prison, design a bridge, or guide a revolution. Ideas gain their force through people, institutions, resources, and practice.

Changing the words on a ministry cannot feed anyone. Changing legal ownership while leaving the old command of work untouched cannot free anyone. The real question is whether the relations governing life are being transformed.

Materialism keeps the programme honest. It asks what people actually do, who controls the conditions, where the product goes, what nature can bear, and whether the result matches the declared purpose.

The people who make society must govern it

Capitalist rule rests on more than owners’ names written on deeds. It is defended through courts, police, prisons, military command, bureaucracy, property law, finance, and habits of obedience built into daily work. The working class cannot simply sit in the old ruler’s chair and expect the machinery to obey a new purpose.

It has to break and transform that machinery while building forms of power rooted in workers’ own activity.

This is Proletarian Self-Governance. Its historical name is the dictatorship of the proletariat, meaning the working class organized as the ruling class during the transition out of capitalism. The phrase names which class holds power. It cannot honestly describe a party, army, or bureaucracy ruling over a silent working class.

Capitalist classes do not usually surrender property, command, and wealth because an assembly asks politely. Counterrevolution, sabotage, foreign pressure, armed resistance, and civil war have shaped real revolutions. Proletarian power must be capable of defending the transformation. Its force serves a class abolishing class rule, and that purpose places a demand on its form: working people must remain the acting political subject.

If a permanent layer gathers secret information, special privileges, armed command, and decision-making into its own hands, a new separation has begun. Calling that layer proletarian does not make workers its rulers.

Self-government therefore needs institutions through which people can act and correct action: workplace bodies, neighborhood communes, assemblies, public services, planning organizations, councils, and accountable delegates. Selection may use elections, recallable appointment, rotation, sortition, or a mixture suited to the task. The decisive qualities are public responsibility, real removability, shared knowledge, and no protected caste.

The Commonalty begins before the state ends

Picture a neighborhood that has taken responsibility for food distribution, childcare, maintenance, public health, and the flow of information to wider planning bodies. These are no longer services handed down by a distant office. They have entered the ordinary competence of society.

This growing capacity is the Commonalty: society directly organizing its shared life.

It begins during the transition. It does not wait behind a finish line marked communism.

At first, a coercive state still exists because classes and organized enemies still exist. People inherit unequal regions, damaged institutions, old property claims, international threats, and generations of training in being ruled. At the same time, the Commonalty takes up every public function that can be turned into shared administration. Knowledge leaves closed departments. Accounts become readable. People rotate through responsibility, develop skill, audit decisions, and connect local experience to wider needs.

As class antagonism disappears, the need for a separate coercive power disappears with it. The useful work once trapped inside the state is absorbed into common life. Coordination remains. Administration remains. Binding decisions remain. A special body standing over society loses its reason to exist.

The state withers because society grows capable of doing consciously what had confronted it as an outside command.

Production learns to speak in needs and materials

The clinic calls for insulin. A farm reports a crop disease. A railway needs steel before typhoon season. Several towns need the same construction crews at once.

Under capitalism, these demands move through separate budgets, contracts, prices, private information, and the ability to pay. Communist production has to connect them directly as parts of one social process.

Associated producers are people who consciously organize their connected labour. They do not encounter one another mainly as private owners exchanging products after the work is done. They begin from the connection.

That requires calculation. A society cannot vote extra copper into the ground or wish a trained surgeon into existence. Planning has to trace materials, skills, time, transport, energy, reserves, ecological costs, and different kinds of need. A shortage needs to travel quickly from the shelf where it appears to everyone whose decision can relieve it.

Computers can reveal patterns, compare options, and find bottlenecks too large for one mind to see. They are instruments of observation. People choose the purposes, judge competing needs, challenge the data, and remain responsible for what is done. No model can decide how much risk a community should bear or whose pain was missing from the numbers used to train it.

A living plan moves in both directions. Common priorities guide local action. Local knowledge corrects the common plan. Mistakes become information instead of secrets to hide from a superior.

Production for use does not promise an infinite cupboard. It replaces the rule of profitable exchange with open responsibility for what can be made, what must be preserved, and who needs what.

A new way of making things makes new people

Communism would be shallow if it changed only where finished goods went.

Work enters the body. It trains the hands, senses, patience, imagination, confidence, and understanding. Work performed under fear, exhaustion, and orders can damage those powers. Work people understand and shape can develop them.

Education can join thought with practical creation instead of training children to endure a lifetime of commands. Socialized childcare can bring care into shared responsibility without stripping families of tenderness or privacy. Quality housing and thoughtful social architecture can give people solitude when they need it and common spaces where life can gather. Shorter necessary labour can return time for friendship, science, music, sport, public responsibility, sleep, and forms of human power that present society leaves unborn.

Common wealth grows as these capacities become available to everyone. Access can increasingly follow need rather than an individual claim measured through money. Equity matters because human beings are different. A society serious about freedom gives more care where more care is needed and removes barriers that equal portions would leave standing.

The purpose is the full development of each person through the free development of all.

Nature keeps the account

Every meal begins in soil, water, sunlight, weather, and living bodies. Every building moves stone, sand, metal, timber, and energy. Every sewer returns something to a river or sea.

Social metabolism is this material interchange between human society and nature. We take, transform, use, discard, restore, and sometimes destroy. Nature receives the result whether an economic account records it or not.

Capitalist firms can treat a poisoned river, a broken climate, or exhausted soil as costs left for somebody else. Communist planning has to count them as changes to the common conditions of life. Production must remain inside the rates at which living systems can renew, while repairing damage already done.

That cannot mean asking poor regions to preserve poverty in the name of a planet damaged mainly through wealthy accumulation. Places deprived by colonialism and imperialism need housing, sanitation, medicine, energy, transport, and productive capacity. Rich regions must cut wasteful throughput and release resources, knowledge, and ecological space. Equity applies between regions as well as between people.

A common world requires a common responsibility for the earth that carries it.

Organization gives memory to struggle

None of these transformations emerges from one brilliant afternoon. A class learns to govern by organizing before, during, and after revolutionary breaks.

Unions and other economic organizations can unite workers around immediate conditions, teach solidarity, and reveal the power hidden in cooperation. Political organization connects those struggles to the abolition of class rule. It preserves lessons, studies changing conditions, develops strategy, and helps people act together across distance and time.

A communist party can contribute knowledge, courage, coordination, and a clear programme. It cannot perform the emancipation of the working class on the class’s behalf. The moment workers become an audience watching specialists make history for them, communist organization has defeated its own purpose.

Organization must carry memory without turning memory into scripture. It must hold a direction without pretending every turn was known in advance. Practice tests strategy. Defeat teaches only when people can tell the truth about it.

One destination, unfinished roads

The destination is settled: class rule ends, production becomes associated and directed toward use, common wealth supports human development, the Commonalty absorbs public life, and the separate state disappears with the conditions that required it.

No honest programme can announce the exact duration of transition, the proper size of every commune, one universal method of delegation, or a planning formula suitable for every place. These depend on productive capacity, geography, inherited institutions, international struggle, ecological damage, and what people learn through practice.

Open design is a strength when the destination remains clear. It lets future people solve problems with knowledge we do not possess. It becomes an excuse only when uncertainty is used to hide the return of wage labour, private accumulation, class command, or rule from above.

Morning after a revolution will arrive. Someone will open the clinic. Someone will check the water. Someone will ask where the grain has gone.

The measure of communism is that these connected acts stop returning to people as the commands of property, money, and a separate power. Society learns to see its own hands in the world, to guide them together, and to make every shared power answer to human life.
