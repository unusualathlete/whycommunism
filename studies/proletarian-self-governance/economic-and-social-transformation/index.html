After the Deed Is Signed

The ownership deed changes at noon. By evening, the factory still has the same machines, supervisors, debts, suppliers, measurements, workday, products, and locked office.

The capitalist no longer owns it.

What relation has taken the capitalist’s place?

This is the first hard question after expropriation. A legal transfer can break private command. It cannot create social control by itself. The old economy may survive inside public property if workers still receive orders from above, enterprises still produce for sale, communities still reach necessities through money, and administrators still judge success through revenue.

Expropriation opens the transformation. It is not the transformation completed.

Four acts hidden inside one word

These terms name different movements:

Term What changes immediately What remains unanswered
expropriation the private owner’s legal power to command labor and resources ends who governs, plans, and receives the product
nationalization ownership passes to a national public body whether workers and communities have real control
collectivization separate producers enter a cooperative or common form whether it is voluntary, supported, coerced, or still market-bound
socialization activity enters real direction by society how far production for use and common provision have developed

A revolutionary decree may contain all four words while accomplishing only the first two.

The distinction matters because property is not merely a thing. It is a social permission. Ownership of a power grid, port, bank, mine, or apartment empire gives some people control over the conditions under which other people work and live.

Expropriation removes that private title to command. Socialization answers the next question: how does the capacity become part of common life?

Concentration changes the path

One formula cannot transform every activity.

A national electrical grid already links millions of lives through one technical system. It cannot be divided into competing neighborhood wires. Its immediate transformation requires coordination at its real scale, along with direct authority for grid workers and public control over investment, access, safety, and ecological purpose.

A worker cooperative begins elsewhere. Its members may already elect managers and control the workplace. Yet it buys inputs and sells products in a market. Competition can still force longer hours, layoffs, cheap materials, or damage shifted onto another community. The cooperative needs to federate into wider supply and planning so its internal democracy is no longer disciplined by exchange.

A family repair shop or small farm may depend on knowledge held by a few people and relationships built over decades. A distant office that absorbs it overnight can destroy useful capacity. Common tools, storage, health care, transport, credit relief, guaranteed access to necessities, and federated planning can make association materially attractive.

The paths differ because the starting relations differ.

Text alternative: After private command ends, large systems enter immediate common coordination, cooperatives federate outward, and small producers integrate through support and expanding common provision. These paths converge on real authority for workers and communities, production for use, and declining dependence on exchange.

Treating every small producer as a capitalist enemy mistakes scale, function, and dependence. Leaving every cooperative to the market mistakes internal ownership for communism. Leaving every nationalized industry to a ministry mistakes state title for social power.

The direction is common. The first steps are not identical.

Production for exchange survives its owner

A public enterprise can continue acting like capital.

It can buy labor-power, sell products, accumulate money, compete for inputs, hide information, and judge every decision by financial return. Its surplus may now enter a state budget instead of a private dividend. The workers can still meet their own social activity as an alien power.

Production for exchange means that labor becomes social through the sale of its product. Society discovers afterward whether separate acts of production count.

Production for use begins with conscious relations before work starts:

  • what is needed;
  • what capacities exist;
  • what materials and ecological limits bind the choice;
  • how burdens are shared;
  • which alternatives are possible; and
  • how results return to change the next plan.

This movement can begin before money has disappeared. A society may inherit wages, taxes, retail exchange, foreign trade, and millions of personal habits built around purchase. Common provision weakens their power by removing necessities from sale.

When water, housing, health, food, education, transport, communication, care, and energy become secure common capacities, money governs less of life.

Money withers through the growth of relations that make it unnecessary.

Several accounts, no sovereign number

Planning cannot replace price with one better number.

An hour of labor, a ton of steel, a hectare of land, a unit of energy, a medical risk, and a damaged river do not become commensurable because a computer can place them in adjacent columns.

A plan needs different accounts:

  • labor accounts show time, intensity, and scarce capacities;
  • physical accounts show material stocks and flows;
  • ecological accounts show sinks, regeneration, damage, and long horizons;
  • capacity accounts show tools, infrastructure, trained people, and bottlenecks; and
  • public judgment chooses purposes and acceptable burdens.

Each corrects the blind spots of the others.

Labor time matters because society must know how much of its life a choice consumes. It cannot decide whether the choice is wanted. Physical feasibility matters because desire cannot create copper or a trained surgical team. Ecological limits matter because a plan cannot negotiate with a collapsing aquifer. Democratic judgment matters because no account can decide what a good life should contain.

Some transitional proposals use labor certificates as a claim on part of the consumer product. Such a certificate does not function as capital if it cannot circulate, purchase means of production, hire labor, earn interest, or accumulate as a transferable fortune. It can still reproduce an equal measure applied to unequal people.

That is why labor accounting and distribution by need cannot be collapsed.

The common product is social before it is individual

Marx’s Critique of the Gotha Programme begins distribution before anyone receives a personal share.

Society first replaces used means of production, expands necessary capacity, holds reserves, and supports administration, schools, health, and people unable to work. These common deductions are not a tax imposed on an otherwise private product. They express the fact that associated labor produces a social whole.

Only then does individual distribution arise.

Even here, equal quantities can produce unequal life. A child, a pregnant person, a diabetic adult, an elder with difficulty chewing, and someone performing intense physical labor have different nutritional needs. The same basket gives formal equality and material inequality.

Equity means organizing access around actual need and capacity so that people can participate in common life.

It is not charity added after an equal distribution fails. It is a different principle of social membership.

The revolution reaches the household

An economy is reproduced in kitchens, bedrooms, clinics, schools, streets, and relationships.

If care remains a private obligation assigned mainly to women, public ownership can coexist with an old gendered division of labor. If housing remains scarce and households depend on one wage, private domination can survive inside the home. If technical education remains restricted, workers can formally own production while a specialist caste governs it. If migration and colonial supply chains remain unequal, a revolutionary center can improve its own life by preserving burdens elsewhere.

Economic and social transformation therefore move together:

  1. common provision reduces dependence on wages and private households;
  2. shorter necessary labor creates time for care, education, government, and free activity;
  3. communal services socialize burdens without standardizing every form of intimacy;
  4. education spreads scientific, technical, and administrative capacity;
  5. transformed space changes access to privacy, association, mobility, and care; and
  6. international coordination prevents one region’s liberation from resting on another’s extraction.

Social revolution is not a cultural decoration placed on an economic base after the serious work is done.

It changes the conditions under which production can become genuinely common.

Direction without a timetable

Some relations can be broken quickly. A landlord’s legal claim can end. A secret corporate account can open. A medicine patent can cease excluding patients. A privileged salary can be abolished.

Other changes require seasons, training, construction, international supply, accumulated trust, and new habits. A society devastated by war cannot move at the pace of one with intact infrastructure. A community with strong popular organs begins from a different position than one in which administration is almost entirely monopolized by the old state.

A rapid movement away from exchange may be possible. It cannot become a calendar guaranteed in advance.

The useful measures are directional:

  • Does common provision cover more of life?
  • Do workers and communities control more of the real decision?
  • Is labor social before sale rather than validated afterward?
  • Are money, rent, and debt losing power over access?
  • Is necessary labor shrinking?
  • Are care and knowledge becoming common capacities?
  • Are ecological and international costs entering the decision?

The deed matters. It breaks the owner’s claim.

Communism begins when society can answer what happens the next morning.
