The Machine Must Never Become System 5

A planning system compares two railway plans.

The first reaches more people this decade. The second protects more habitat and leaves room for a future line. Both fit the available steel, labour, energy, and construction time. The model reports that the first plan moves more passengers per hour.

That is useful.

It is not a decision.

The number cannot tell people how much immediate access should weigh against ecological repair, future flexibility, or the life of a neighbourhood. It can trace consequences inside purposes people have already chosen. It cannot become the author of those purposes.

The principle is exact:

AI can absorb variety. It must not exercise judgment or political rule.

A model is a useful reduction

A model is a representation made for a purpose.

A railway map removes houses, trees, soil, and weather so routes become visible. A production model may remove the colour of a machine and retain its capacity, inputs, failures, and repair time. A language model may compress patterns in an enormous collection of text so it can generate likely continuations.

Reduction creates usefulness.

Reduction also creates a boundary. The model can answer only through what entered its representation, the relations it learned, and the objective it was given.

No model contains the whole world. If it did, it would no longer simplify anything enough to help.

Computation enlarges human attention

A complex plan may connect millions of goods, processes, workers, machines, routes, time periods, and ecological conditions.

No person can hold all those relations in memory.

Computation can:

  • balance large physical accounts;
  • trace which inputs depend on which others;
  • compare many feasible plans;
  • search for bottlenecks;
  • forecast changing demand;
  • notice an unusual pattern;
  • test how an assumption changes a result;
  • update a schedule after a disruption; and
  • show where actual outcomes differ from the plan.

Cybernetics calls this variety absorption. The tool helps an organization respond to more possible conditions than unaided attention could manage.

The machine extends sight.

The people looking still need to know what deserves attention.

Prediction remains an if

A prediction has the form: if these conditions hold, this result is likely.

If a clinic receives two more nurses, waiting times may fall. If a region redirects steel, one rail project may finish sooner. If drought reduces a crop, several substitutes may preserve nutrition.

Every prediction depends on conditions that can change.

Some conditions may be missing from the model. A rural clinic can appear to have low demand because a bridge washed out and patients cannot reach it. A workshop can appear inefficient because it received defective material. A fast process can appear superior because the account did not include injuries or repair.

The model can identify a pattern in records. It cannot assume the pattern explains itself.

The average firm is not this firm

A wider planning body can learn a great deal about a class of workplaces.

It can know typical inputs, outputs, machinery, labour time, failure rates, and maintenance. Better computation can make that knowledge richer.

Knowing a particular workplace still requires study of that workplace.

The same fall in output can mean:

  • broken equipment;
  • missing inputs;
  • a safer method;
  • exhausted workers;
  • false reporting;
  • a changed product;
  • an environmental disturbance; or
  • a useful experiment that temporarily slowed production.

An AI system may rank the possible causes and show which evidence fits each. Direct experience and audit discover what is actually happening.

This is why greater information at a higher recursion does not dissolve operational autonomy. The people inside the process remain producers of knowledge, not merely objects described from above.

Discrepancy is a question

Suppose four workshops use similar machinery and labour. One produces more reliable pumps with fewer hours and fewer injuries.

A model can flag the difference.

It cannot know from the difference alone whether the cause is a measurement error, an easier batch of material, hidden overtime, or a better technique.

The proper response is a question: what happened here?

An audit can study the actual process. If workers developed a labour-saving method, the system can test and spread it. If the records were wrong, the account can be repaired. If local conditions explain the difference, the comparison can be corrected.

Computation helps society notice where learning is needed. It should not convert every anomaly into guilt or every correlation into an order.

System 3 and System 4 can use powerful tools

In the Viable System Model, System 3 coordinates present internal activity. It balances current resources, commitments, and performance.

System 4 studies the environment and future. It follows scientific change, ecological risk, new needs, and possible paths.

Computation can support both.

For System 3, models can help schedule connected work, trace shortages, compare actual and expected results, and reveal conflicts among present commitments.

For System 4, models can compare long scenarios, examine uncertainty, forecast ecological change, and test how one investment alters future capacity.

These are immense powers.

They remain powers of analysis.

System 5 answers a different question

System 5 holds identity, purpose, and policy.

It asks:

  • What is this organization for?
  • Which human needs define success?
  • Which principles cannot be traded away?
  • How should present operation be balanced against future development?
  • What sacrifices may society ask, and from whom?
  • Which feasible future should become the common plan?

No increase in processing speed turns these into technical questions.

System 5 must remain separate from System 3. The apparatus managing today’s operations must not quietly acquire the right to define tomorrow’s purpose. If the planning engine supplies the objectives, evaluates the results, and issues the decisions, technical control has swallowed politics.

At the broadest recursion, System 5 belongs to the self-governing Commonalty. Elected and recallable public servants can carry functions. Specialists can explain consequences. Models can compare possibilities. Associated people remain the authors of the purpose.

Capability creates no title to rule

Present AI systems are limited. Future systems may become far more capable.

The political principle does not depend on today’s limitations.

The argument goes further than saying current models are inaccurate. Even an artificial general intelligence should not make these decisions because its needs would differ from human needs.

Intelligence is an ability. Political legitimacy is a relation among beings whose common life is being governed.

A brilliant engineer does not gain the right to decide where everyone must live. A perfect calculator does not gain the right to decide which need deserves sacrifice. Knowledge can command attention without commanding people.

If a future artificial system developed interests of its own, that would make the political problem more complicated. It would not make the system an automatic representative of humanity.

Human rule is not a decorative final click

Putting a person beside a machine does not guarantee human judgment.

If the person has no time to examine assumptions, no power to choose another plan, and no path for hearing workers and users, the “human decision” is a signature beneath an automated command.

Real human rule requires institutions capable of:

  • setting the social purpose before computation begins;
  • receiving several feasible alternatives rather than one verdict;
  • hearing direct knowledge from affected workplaces and communities;
  • asking which assumptions produced the result;
  • stopping or revising a proposal;
  • assigning responsibility for the final decision; and
  • learning from actual outcomes.

Marx’s Commune matters here because it brings public functions down to earth. Officials are removable servants under supervision, not a caste whose access to technical knowledge places them above society.

The point is accountable decision, not the ceremonial presence of a human face.

A good model returns alternatives

The proper output of a planning model is not:

WORKED TEMPLATE

Do this.

It is closer to:

Staged plan Yes Preserves flexibility Slower completion Steel supply Whether temporary service is adequate

The model makes consequences easier to inspect. Local reports and investigation correct the representation. Public institutions decide which path becomes binding.

Uncertainty belongs in the answer. A range, a missing fact, or a disputed assumption tells society where caution, reserve, experiment, and further study are needed.

False precision is not intelligence.

The settled line and the open work

Many technical questions remain open.

Which models compare large plans most effectively? How should several physical and labour accounts be connected? When does a prediction become reliable enough for action? How can one model be checked against another? What kinds of anomaly deserve direct investigation?

These require research, experiment, and institutional learning.

The political line is already clear:

  • computation supports calculation;
  • models support Systems 3 and 4;
  • anomalies trigger questions rather than automatic condemnation;
  • knowledge of a class does not replace experience of a particular;
  • capability does not confer a mandate; and
  • AI never becomes System 5.

The open technical programme begins inside that boundary.

The machine beside us

The railway model returns three feasible plans.

It has traced steel, labour, travel, power, construction sequence, and probable use with a range no individual mind could match. It has done something wonderful.

Then it stops where politics begins.

People examine the alternatives, listen to those who will build and live beside the railway, argue about the future, and accept responsibility for a common choice.

The machine stands beside them, extending their sight.

It does not sit above them, deciding what human life is for.
