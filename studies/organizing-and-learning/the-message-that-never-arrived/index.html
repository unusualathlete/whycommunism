The Message That Never Arrived

A branch reports that a campaign is failing.

The regional office softens the report because it fears looking incompetent. The center sees progress and repeats the plan. Branch members create a private channel because the official one has become useless. The center discovers the channel and treats it as proof of disloyalty.

Punishment creates more secrecy.

Secrecy confirms the center’s fear.

The organization has begun eating the trust and knowledge it needs in order to live.

Administration becomes a social power

Administration is necessary. Somebody must keep records, connect schedules, transmit decisions, preserve continuity, and allocate shared resources.

Bureaucracy appears when this necessary activity becomes the private domain of a group able to control:

  • which information counts;
  • which rule applies;
  • who reaches a decision;
  • which resource moves;
  • which failure becomes visible; and
  • who can appeal.

The mechanism does not require evil people.

An office translates complex reports. Translation gives it discretion. Procedure becomes a measure of success. Access to procedure makes the office indispensable. The office begins protecting the conditions that make it indispensable.

The problem is not paper. It is command over the path between knowledge and action.

A faction can be wrong and still be evidence

A faction is an organized current inside a larger organization. It may defend a political line, a leader, a social interest, or control of the apparatus.

Some factions lie, intimidate, sabotage, or seek private advantage. Responsibility remains real.

The existence of misconduct does not finish the causal analysis.

Ask what made a private current useful to its members. Were reports filtered? Was a strategic failure protected from review? Did one group control appointments? Were ordinary routes of disagreement closed?

Trotsky wrote in The New Course that where two opinions exist, people inevitably group. A ban on grouping cannot abolish the conditions producing the two opinions. It can drive the relation underground, where evidence and accountability become weaker.

The faction may be guilty.

It may also be a symptom.

When purpose changes

Organizational autophagy begins when internal survival displaces the class function for which the organization exists.

The class function requires Autophagy rewards
accurate bad news reports that protect careers
criticism that changes practice criticism that identifies enemies
disciplined common action obedience that hides contradiction
leaders who can be corrected leaders whose prestige depends on being right
shared education knowledge hoarded by camps
political unity victory over rivals

The language of programme may remain. The activity reveals the changed purpose.

A center protects its authority. A minority protects its identity. Each action by one justifies a stronger action by the other. Members spend more time interpreting internal signals than understanding the class.

The organization lives on itself.

Penal unity deepens the loop

Mechanical cures target the visible group:

  • dissolve the faction;
  • remove officers;
  • forbid informal communication;
  • require loyalty declarations;
  • stage criticism sessions; or
  • centralize another function.

Any of these measures might be justified in a specific case. None repairs the cause by itself.

If official reports still hide failure, the next informal channel will appear. If office still depends on loyalty, the next career group will form. If criticism still carries punishment, the next error will be concealed.

Compulsory self-criticism can make this worse. An honest member may struggle to explain a mistake. A skilled opportunist may perform the perfect confession and change nothing.

Conduct and changed relations prove correction.

Systemic repair

Repair begins by recovering the real sequence:

flowchart TD
    A["Recover original reports and decisions"] --> B["Make alterations and delays traceable"]
    B --> C["State the political disagreement plainly"]
    C --> D["Identify interests, harms, and blocked channels"]
    D --> E["Set evidence that can test each claim"]
    E --> F["Change the communication or authority relation"]
    F --> G["Review conduct and hold actual harm accountable"]
    G --> H["Test whether common class work improves"]

Text alternative: Repair recovers the record, makes filtering visible, states the disagreement, identifies interests and blocked channels, sets evidence, changes the relation, holds actual harm accountable, and tests whether the organization becomes more capable of its class purpose.

Systemic explanation does not excuse wrongdoing. It prevents punishment from becoming an alibi for leaving the institution unchanged.

Sometimes repair reveals a real programmatic break. No meeting method can unite organizations pursuing incompatible purposes. Separation may be more honest than unity preserved through coercion.

The judgment depends on the programme, social interests, conduct, history, and likely consequences of unity or division.

The hard question

Ask:

WORKED TEMPLATE

Is this conflict making the organization more capable of serving its class purpose, or is the organization feeding on itself to preserve its present form?

If it is feeding on itself, calls for discipline are too small.

Restore the record. Open the blocked channels. Change the offices that make concealment useful. Hold actual harm accountable. Preserve principled disagreement. Dissolve a form that cannot be repaired.

An organization survives by learning what it did not want to hear.
