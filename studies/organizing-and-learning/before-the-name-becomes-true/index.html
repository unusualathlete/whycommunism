Before the Name Becomes True

Twelve people create a logo, publish a programme, elect a central committee, and call themselves the party.

What changed outside the document?

Perhaps nothing.

A communist party is not made by permission, ancestry, sincerity, or a famous name. It exists when an organization can perform the political functions through which the advanced part of the working class prepares, learns, coordinates, and acts around a communist programme.

The name may arrive before the capacity.

The task is to make the name true without pretending it already is.

Four stages, no automatic ladder

Circle

A circle studies, discusses, translates, or publishes. It may perform excellent work. Its continuity may depend on a few people, and its relation to class struggle may be thin.

Calling it a circle is not an insult. False scale is more dangerous than small scale.

Preparatory communist organization

The group develops a coherent programme, regular work, records, training, division of labor, and contact with living struggles. It can survive the absence of one founder.

Party embryo

Several capacities become durable across places and problems. The organization develops militants, investigates conditions, connects struggles, preserves political continuity, and tests strategy and tactics.

Party performing mature functions

The organization is a real political force inside the class. It can coordinate at the necessary scale, maintain international continuity, act under crisis, educate without substituting, and revise tactics while preserving programme.

The movement is not guaranteed. An embryo can die. A mature organization can dissolve into circles while keeping its offices.

Seven tests

  1. Programme: Does the organization carry a coherent communist direction that explains present conditions?
  2. Continuity: Can work survive changes of mood, repression, and the loss of one person?
  3. Life inside the class: Do workers appear as participants and sources of knowledge rather than an audience?
  4. Political development: Can immediate struggles grow toward class independence and power?
  5. Pedagogy: Do members become more capable of investigating, teaching, deciding, and acting?
  6. Strategy, tactics, and feedback: Can the organization judge conditions, act, learn, and revise?
  7. Anti-substitution: Do unions, councils, communes, and other class bodies remain independently alive?

No single answer proves party existence.

The pattern matters.

A group may have a strong text and no organized continuity. It may have disciplined continuity and no living class relation. It may lead a strike and lack an international programme. The honest description tells it what to build next.

Prefiguration without the miniature utopia

An organization inside capitalism cannot become a finished communist society.

Its members still sell labor-power, pay rent, use money, live under law, and carry habits formed by the world they oppose. A shared kitchen does not abolish the commodity. A cooperative office does not abolish class.

Pretending otherwise can turn the group inward. Internal lifestyle becomes a measure of political purity. The organization spends its energy maintaining a miniature world while the class relation outside it remains untouched.

Prefiguration still contains a necessary truth.

Means shape people and institutions. An organization that practices careerism, private privilege, mercantilism, manipulation, and substitution trains capacities hostile to communism.

Negative prefiguration begins by refusing those relations where the organization can:

  • office does not purchase a separate standard of life;
  • political knowledge does not become personal property;
  • skilled people train successors;
  • necessary authority follows function and remains accountable;
  • care work does not disappear into private sacrifice;
  • criticism can alter practice;
  • class organizations are not treated as party possessions; and
  • the organization prepares its own functions to become common capacities.

This is not communism in one meeting.

It is refusing to build the opposite into the instrument.

Function should carry authority

The electrician should lead the electrical work because the work requires knowledge. That leadership should train others, give reasons, and remain bounded by the task.

A political editor should decide whether a text expresses the agreed programme. The editor should not control housing, money, or membership because of that competence.

Authority becomes alienated when one necessary function expands into a general claim over people.

The party minimum is therefore more than having correct offices. It is the ability to place authority where work requires it, keep purpose publicly governed, and spread the knowledge that makes exclusive authority less necessary.

The disappearance test

Communist organization concentrates capacities because they are not yet general:

  • theory;
  • memory;
  • coordination;
  • pedagogy;
  • strategy;
  • administration; and
  • conscious direction.

Its work should spread them.

A party that makes itself permanently indispensable has failed its final test. A party that helps the class acquire its capacities prepares a society in which no separate party function remains.

Before claiming the name, ask what the organization can actually do.

After gaining the capacity, ask whether its activity makes that capacity more common.

The first question prevents fantasy.

The second prevents a throne.
