The Name of a Tactic Proves Nothing

In 1905, Russian revolutionaries argued for boycotting a new parliament. In 1907, some of the same revolutionaries opposed another boycott.

The word did not change.

The relation did.

In 1905, strikes, peasant revolt, military mutiny, and open conflict over state power were pulling political life beyond the parliament. Refusal could concentrate energy in a rising revolutionary movement.

By 1907, repression had broken much of that movement. Refusal risked withdrawing revolutionaries from a field the defeated class still needed to use.

“Boycott” names an act. It does not tell us what the act does.

Three kinds of direction

Principle states the relation that must not be surrendered without surrendering the communist project itself.

Working-class self-emancipation is a principle. Class independence is a principle. Internationalism is a principle. The abolition of class rule is a principle.

Strategy proposes a broad path through an actual balance of forces. It joins several struggles and capacities toward a change in power.

Tactic is a bounded action inside that path: a strike, boycott, election intervention, occupation, publication, retreat, alliance, refusal, or clandestine measure.

The distinction can be shown as a chain:

flowchart LR
    A["Principle:
what relation must hold?"] --> B["Strategy:
what broad development is possible?"]
    B --> C["Tactic:
what action advances it here?"]
    C --> D["Consequence:
what capacity and relation actually changed?"]
    D --> E["Feedback:
what must be revised?"]
    E --> B

Text alternative: Principle establishes the relation that must hold. Strategy proposes a broad development under current conditions. Tactics act within it. Consequences reveal what changed, and feedback revises strategy and later tactics without casually discarding principle.

Principle without strategy becomes a declaration.

Strategy without tactics becomes a forecast.

Tactics without either become motion.

Strategy is a hypothesis

A strategy should be written so reality can defeat it.

WORKED TEMPLATE

If these classes and organizations act through this sequence, under these conditions, then this capacity can grow and this relation of power can be broken, because these material tendencies make the path possible.

This form forces six questions:

  1. What development is expected?
  2. Through which organizations and struggles?
  3. What conditions must remain true?
  4. What evidence shows the route is closing?
  5. Who reviews the assessment?
  6. What people, knowledge, and organization must survive if it fails?

Revision is not automatically betrayal. Refusing to revise a defeated path can sacrifice the movement to the prestige of its leadership.

Revision becomes opportunism when immediate advantage quietly changes the principle or final aim.

Conditions include the organization itself

Objective conditions include the structure of capital, state capacity, war, crisis, law, geography, technology, and the balance among classes.

Subjective conditions include organization, consciousness, trust, discipline, preparation, morale, and the ability to learn.

The distinction is useful but porous. Organization is built by people and becomes an objective fact confronting the next decision. A repression law is objective, but its effect depends on whether a movement prepared secure communication and public support.

A tactic that might be sound for a large, trusted organization can destroy a small one. The same strike can educate or exhaust. The same secrecy can protect necessary work or create an unaccountable internal caste.

Conditions do not excuse judgment. They are what judgment is about.

Follow three outcomes

Every tactic produces more than its announced result.

Outcome Question
external Did it alter the employer, state, institution, or public situation?
organizational Did it increase or reduce capacity, trust, knowledge, and coordination?
formative What habits and relations did participants learn by doing it?

A demonstration may fail to win its demand but build contacts that make a later strike possible. A spectacular action may gain attention while exposing members, isolating the organization, and teaching everyone to wait for a daring minority.

Means educate.

The result therefore cannot be measured by publicity alone, by moral purity, or by whether the act resembles a famous revolutionary episode.

A field judgment

Before acting, state:

  • the purpose;
  • the principle that limits the act;
  • the strategic capacity it should develop;
  • the objective and subjective conditions;
  • the people carrying the greatest risk;
  • likely external, organizational, and formative outcomes;
  • the sign that means continue;
  • the sign that means alter or stop; and
  • the body that receives reports and can change the action.

After acting, compare the result with the chain.

A tactic can then be rejected as harmful, rejected as self-consuming, redesigned, tested under stated conditions, or supported under stated conditions.

These are judgments, not permanent labels attached to a method.

The point of flexibility

Tactical flexibility is often defended as freedom for leaders. Its communist purpose is different.

Conditions change. A movement needs enough memory to recognize the change and enough feedback to alter its action without losing its direction.

The principle is not a frozen phrase. It is the relation that lets action remain part of emancipation.

The strategy is not prophecy. It is an organized wager answerable to evidence.

The tactic is not good because of its name.

It is good only if people, acting under these conditions, can use it to become more capable of changing the relation they set out to abolish.
