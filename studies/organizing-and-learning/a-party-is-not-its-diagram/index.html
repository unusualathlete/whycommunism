A Party Is Not Its Diagram

Five groups agree to distribute the same political text on the same morning.

One reaches hundreds of workers. One arrives after the shift. One discovers the gate moved months ago. One never receives the final file. One reports success because its members fear blame.

The chart shows a center and five branches.

The organization that acted contained languages, schedules, tired people, unreliable platforms, unknown security rules, printing limits, and different levels of trust.

The chart was true and useless.

Begin with the living system

An organization is a relation among:

  • purpose;
  • people;
  • work;
  • knowledge;
  • resources;
  • authority;
  • communication;
  • an environment; and
  • ways to detect and correct error.

The official description may say, “The center sends a file to five branches.”

The living description says, “One editor finishes late, sends through an unreliable platform, and expects five differently situated groups to translate, print, travel, enter, speak, and report.”

The second description can be changed because it names the mechanism.

Bogdanov’s wager

Alexander Bogdanov’s tektology studied patterns of organization across different kinds of systems. Its wager was that combining, separating, stabilizing, disrupting, and reorganizing share discoverable relations.

The political use is not to treat people like machine parts. It is to stop treating every organizational failure as a moral surprise.

Ask:

  • Did the form serve the purpose?
  • Which boundary blocked necessary knowledge?
  • Which difference did the center flatten?
  • Which function depended on one irreplaceable person?
  • Did the result alter the next action?

The object of study is the relation producing the conduct.

Variety has to meet variety

Stafford Beer used the term variety for the range of states a system may encounter or produce. An organization needs enough response capacity to handle the differences that matter.

The five sites differ by language, access, shift, security, and local politics. One identical instruction cannot absorb all of them.

Unlimited local variation also fails. A group cannot rewrite the programme because the gate moved.

The organization must decide:

Preserve locally Coordinate commonly
language and examples political meaning
route and timing common date where simultaneity matters
response to gate conditions safety and reporting standards
conversation method strategic purpose

This is neither micromanagement nor independence without obligation.

It is designed autonomy within a common act.

Feedback closes the loop

“Distribution completed” reports obedience. It does not report reality.

Useful feedback says where contact happened, what workers asked, which resource failed, what the group changed, and what should change next.

Information must move:

  • upward, so wider bodies can learn from local activity;
  • downward, so local bodies know the whole result and reasons for revision;
  • sideways, so groups can compare methods directly; and
  • outward, so the organization hears the class and environment it is trying to affect.

If information only climbs, branches become sensors for someone else’s intelligence. If it only circulates locally, the movement cannot combine what it knows.

Feedback is complete only when someone capable of changing the next action receives it.

Levels rhyme without copying

A base group, city organization, national center, and International all need purpose, activity, information, coordination, and correction.

Their forms should differ because their environments differ.

A local group needs dense knowledge of workplaces and people. A national organ needs to compare regions, preserve programme, and coordinate resources. An International needs to understand world capital and connect movements across states.

A movement cannot be scaled with a photocopier.

Recursion means each level is a viable whole inside a larger whole. It does not mean each level owns a smaller replica of the central office.

Audit can search for success

The branch that changed the translation created far more discussion.

An audit should not assume deviation caused the problem. It compares texts, speaks with workers, and asks what the group understood.

Perhaps the central draft was abstract. Perhaps the new version also introduced an error. Both can be true.

Audit should reveal:

  1. what happened;
  2. what relation produced it;
  3. what change to test; and
  4. whether the change improved the next result.

An inspection system that only punishes failure teaches concealment. One that also discovers a superior method can turn local intelligence into common capacity.

The model does not choose the programme

Cybernetics can make a corporation, army, prison, hospital, or communist organization more effective.

Efficiency does not settle purpose.

A communist organization must ask which class it serves, what relation it seeks to abolish, what must remain under members’ and the class’s control, and what kind of people its methods produce.

No model answers those questions by itself.

Organizational science can show that the file arrived late. It can expose a bottleneck and test a new channel. It cannot decide whether the organization should distribute that political line, whether the line serves working-class emancipation, or whether the risk is justified.

The party is not its diagram.

It is a living political relation that must become capable of seeing and changing its own form without allowing the form to choose its purpose.
