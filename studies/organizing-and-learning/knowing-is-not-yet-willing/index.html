Knowing Is Not Yet Willing

A room can agree that a schedule is unsafe.

Agreement does not stop the buses.

Some workers fear dismissal. Some expect somebody else to move first. Some know the last stoppage failed. Riders depend on the route for hospital appointments. Agency workers do not share the same legal protection. Everyone may understand the problem and still lack the power to act.

Knowledge becomes political will only when people can carry it together.

Consciousness is a social capacity

Class consciousness is often pictured as a correct idea entering many individual heads.

The class exists through relations that no individual can experience whole. A driver knows the schedule. A mechanic knows the brake failures. A rider knows the missed connection. A dispatcher knows the staffing gap. A communist organizer may know the history of transport strikes and privatization.

Consciousness develops when these fragments become a common account linked to common action.

WORKED TEMPLATE

understanding guides activity; activity tests understanding; organization preserves what people learn; changed capacity makes new activity possible.

This is praxis: thought and action changing the world and the people acting in it.

The educator enters the problem

Marx’s third thesis on Feuerbach warns that the educator must be educated.

This does not mean every claim is equally correct. It means political knowledge cannot be delivered from a position outside social life.

An organizer may bring theory, comparative history, legal knowledge, and wider connections. Workers and residents bring knowledge of the actual system, its hidden pressures, and the people who must carry the decision.

Political pedagogy joins them through a process:

  1. state a real problem;
  2. gather the different questions inside it;
  3. map power, capacity, risk, and missing knowledge;
  4. compare experience with historical and theoretical understanding;
  5. choose an action;
  6. record what would count as success, danger, or failure; and
  7. return after action to revise the shared account.

A lecture can be part of this process. It cannot replace it.

Will is organized endurance

Political will is class consciousness becoming durable collective action.

It includes conviction, but conviction alone is fragile. A person may feel certain at midnight and remain silent at work the next morning.

Will needs material supports:

  • relationships strong enough to survive fear;
  • organization that can convert agreement into tasks;
  • memory of earlier victories and defeats;
  • care for people carrying risk;
  • training that reduces dependence on one expert;
  • review that can admit error without dissolving purpose; and
  • practices repeated until they become ordinary conduct.

The quiet years matter. A movement that exists only during crisis has to invent trust while events are moving fastest.

Orthopraxy without obedience

Orthopraxy means carrying conviction into repeated practice.

The term can sound like conformity. Here it names ordinary acts that make communist relations more possible:

  • beginning review with facts rather than blame;
  • rotating a skilled task while training a successor;
  • recording a minority warning;
  • caring for a member during illness;
  • refusing career privilege;
  • returning after failure to repair harm; and
  • treating a worker as a source of knowledge rather than an audience.

Repeated practice shapes character. It can also become empty ritual.

The test is whether the practice preserves its purpose and remains answerable to consequence.

Criticism and self-criticism can help people correct conduct. Forced confession can train verbal submission. Discipline can join action. Obedience can hide error. A study circle can develop militants. Vocabulary can become a rank.

Formative practice has to form people capable of judgment, not people skilled at displaying agreement.

Neither spontaneity nor will alone

Material crisis creates openings. It does not write the programme, preserve memory, build trust, or coordinate action.

Will creates initiative. It cannot wish away a hostile balance of forces, missing organization, exhausted people, or absent resources.

The false choice is:

  • conditions will force consciousness into being; or
  • sufficient conviction can overcome any condition.

Praxis changes both sides. Organization acts within conditions and becomes part of the conditions confronting the next action.

People do not wait to become perfect revolutionaries before acting. They become more capable through action organized so that experience can be understood and preserved.

The unfinished edge

No method guarantees a communist will.

People can learn domination through struggle. Sacrifice can become a cult. Loyalty can protect friends from criticism. A courageous minority can begin treating courage as a claim to command.

Political education must therefore ask what conduct the organization is producing.

Are more people able to investigate, decide, teach, and act? Can conviction survive disagreement? Does discipline carry a common decision while preserving the information needed to correct it? Can members remain human under pressure rather than converting hardness into virtue?

Knowing is not yet willing.

Willing is not yet succeeding.

The bridge between them is an organization that turns experience into common understanding, common understanding into action, and action back into a deeper capacity to choose.

Source notes

  • Karl Marx, “Theses on Feuerbach”, 1845, especially theses 3 and 11.
  • Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels, The German Ideology, Part I, written 1845 to 1846, on material life, consciousness, and revolutionary transformation.
  • Ernesto Che Guevara, “On Revolutionary Medicine”, 1960, on learning and transformation through service with the people.
  • Alexander Bogdanov, Tektology: Universal Organization Science, on learning, organization, and the reorganization of collective experience.
