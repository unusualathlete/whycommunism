WHY COMMUNISM? GUIDE · Proletarian Self-Governance
11Proletarian Self-Governance

No Country Can Finish the Revolution Alone

A vial of insulin can contain chemicals refined in one country, machinery built in another, glass made in a third, and research gathered across generations. A hospital may stand inside one city while the conditions of its work stretch around the planet.

Capitalism did this. It tore production out of local isolation, joined distant labor through the world market, and made every country dependent on work performed elsewhere. It also arranged that dependence through conquest, debt, unequal exchange, colonial borders, military force, and the private command of capital.

The revolution begins somewhere. Communism cannot end at that border.

The world market made a world-historical class

The modern proletariat is world-historical because its life is directly joined to a world system of production and struggle.

A dock strike can halt factories across an ocean. A mine closure can interrupt batteries, hospitals, and railways. A financial panic can destroy work in countries whose people never made the decisions that caused it. Workers compete across borders because capital can move, compare wages, reorganize supply chains, and make one population’s desperation a weapon against another.

The same system creates the material possibility of overcoming that competition. Production already happens through international cooperation. The knowledge, infrastructure, and habits required for a common world economy have been built inside capitalism, though in a violent and distorted form.

World revolution does not impose connection upon separate peoples. It takes a connection that already exists and changes who controls it.

Isolation bends the revolution back toward capital

An isolated proletarian state remains surrounded by the world market. It needs medicines, machine parts, knowledge, energy, and trade. It faces military pressure, blocked credit, sanctions, sabotage, and the constant threat that scarcity will turn every political question into an emergency.

Those pressures have a direction.

Pressure on an isolated revolution What it can reproduce
military encirclement permanent command, secrecy, and a standing emergency apparatus
dependence on foreign currency production for export instead of common need
technological isolation a privileged technical caste controlling scarce knowledge
unequal trade intensified labor at home to obtain necessities abroad
competition with capitalist states accumulation for national power rather than human development
blockade and shortage rationing administered above the population instead of through it

None of this proves that a revolution should wait until every country moves at once. Waiting would leave initiative to capital forever. It proves that a victory in one place must use every advance to widen the struggle, build material solidarity, and reduce the isolation that deforms it.

Domestic transformation and international expansion are one movement. Expropriation at home weakens capital. International support protects expropriation. Each new revolution opens knowledge, resources, territory, and human capacity to the others.

A world republic begins even within one country

The proletarian revolution fights on national terrain because states, armies, law, and property are organized there. Its class is not national.

A worker does not become politically foreign by crossing a border. Migrants, refugees, and people from oppressed nations belong to the same world class. A proletarian republic must recognize that fact in its institutions, public life, and right to participate.

This is what it means to say that the world republic already appears within the national revolution. The revolution governs a definite territory while refusing to define its human community by nationality. Its border marks a material limit of present power, not the moral limit of solidarity.

National cultures, languages, and histories do not need to disappear. What must disappear is the power of one nation to enrich itself by dominating another.

Internationalism has to change material life

Proletarian internationalism is the organization of working-class power across borders.

Solidarity begins with words and becomes real through action:

  • workers resist their own ruling class when it attacks a revolution abroad;
  • movements share knowledge, equipment, refuge, communications, and medical aid;
  • richer regions transfer productive capacity without turning assistance into debt or control;
  • revolutionary organizations coordinate strategy against capital that already acts internationally;
  • oppressed peoples receive direct support against colonial and imperial domination;
  • common defense prevents one isolated population from carrying the whole military burden;
  • international planning repairs uneven development instead of using it.

Formal equality between unequal countries preserves inequality. A region stripped of minerals, infrastructure, land, and trained labor does not become equal because a treaty gives every delegation one vote. Equity requires rebuilding the productive and human capacities that colonialism deliberately distorted.

That support cannot reproduce empire under a red flag. The people receiving resources must retain power over how development changes their land and life. International planning earns unity by making liberation tangible.

The Communist International is an organ of common struggle

A Communist International joins revolutionary parties, workers’ organizations, and proletarian states around a common programme.

It is more than a conference and less than a sovereign standing above the world proletariat. Its purpose is practical:

  1. combine struggles that capital tries to isolate;
  2. share the accumulated experience of victories and defeats;
  3. coordinate political and material support;
  4. prevent a national party or state from treating its temporary interest as the interest of the whole class;
  5. direct attention and resources toward the places where world struggle can advance;
  6. maintain a common communist horizon through changing local conditions.

The International must understand differences. A strike in an imperial center, an anti-colonial uprising, a revolution under blockade, and a movement organizing migrant labor do not face identical tasks.

Unity does not require copying one form everywhere. It requires every form to serve the emancipation of the world proletariat.

The International itself needs mandates, recall, transparent reasons, and direct communication with the organizations that compose it. International coordination can also become alienated power. A headquarters that cannot be corrected by workers has reproduced the problem at a larger scale.

From federation to a world republic

The world republic is the global federation of self-governing commonalties.

It grows as revolutions join their production, knowledge, defense, and ecological life. Early federations may still contain states, currencies, borders, and unequal transitional forms. Their work is to remove the material basis of those separations.

The scale of each decision follows the relation being governed:

Common relation Necessary scale
a neighborhood meal service commune or local federation
a watershed crossing several regions all communities and producers within the watershed
an electrical interconnection the regions linked by the grid
a pandemic coordinated public health across the world
the climate and oceans planetary planning
language, custom, and local public space the communities living them

The world republic does not mean one office deciding everything. It means no private capital or rival state can block the rational coordination of things that are already global.

Food reserves can move before a famine becomes a market opportunity. Scientific knowledge can circulate without patents. Production can be located according to ecology, human need, and total labor rather than cheap wages. Public transportation can connect regions without serving profitable routes alone. Climate repair can become a common labor of humanity.

The local does not vanish into the universal. Local self-government gains the world as its field of cooperation.

Any international institution can speak of unity. Its material direction reveals what kind of unity it creates.

Real internationalism weakens the domination of rich regions over poor ones. It lets workers and communities correct the bodies coordinating them. Knowledge moves freely instead of becoming leverage held by specialists or powerful states. Common defense protects revolution without creating a permanent military authority. Resources move toward equal human development rather than national prestige. Each advance enlarges the capacity of people everywhere to govern their own common life.

The world market united humanity behind our backs. Prices, debt, corporations, and states make decisions whose causes are scattered and whose costs arrive without consent.

The world republic brings that unity under conscious human direction.

No country can finish the revolution alone because no country contains the relations that must be transformed. The working class becomes capable of abolishing the world market only by acting as the world class capitalism has already made.

Source notes

Primary texts

  • Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels, The German Ideology, Part I, written 1845 to 1846. Its account of the world market explains why the proletariat and communism can exist only as world-historical forces.
  • Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels, Manifesto of the Communist Party, 1848. It connects the world market, national antagonism, proletarian political power, and the association in which the free development of each supports the free development of all.
  • Karl Marx, General Rules of the International Working Men’s Association, revised 1871 from the 1864 Provisional Rules. It makes working-class emancipation the work of the class itself and gives international organization a concrete federative form.
  • First Congress of the Communist International, The Platform of the Communist International, March 6, 1919. It treats capitalist crisis and proletarian revolution as a single world process requiring international organization.
  • V. I. Lenin, Draft Theses on National and Colonial Questions, June 5, 1920. It distinguishes oppressed from oppressor nations and insists that formal national equality cannot replace material liberation.

Further study

  • Rosa Luxemburg, “The Russian Tragedy”, September 1918. It shows how international failure can force an isolated revolution into concessions that cannot be understood as a purely national choice.
  • Second Congress of the Communist International, Report of the Commission on the National and Colonial Questions, July 26, 1920. The debate makes concrete economic conditions, colonial domination, and the agency of oppressed peoples central to international strategy.
