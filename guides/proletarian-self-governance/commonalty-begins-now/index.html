08Proletarian Self-Governance

The Commonalty Does Not Wait

The Commonalty is sometimes pictured at the end of a long tunnel. First comes revolution, then a transitional state, and only after the state disappears does society finally govern itself.

That sequence is wrong.

If working people do not begin governing from the first day, no later authority can deliver self-government to them. The Commonalty appears wherever people organize common life directly. At first it is partial, uneven, and surrounded by inherited class relations. It grows through the transition until a separate power over society has nothing left to do.

The transition is the Commonalty becoming universal.

Gemeinwesen means organized common being. The German word can mean community, common being, or commonalty. In 1875, Engels proposed it as a better term than “state” for the political form opened by the Paris Commune. He wanted to distinguish a community organizing itself from a state standing “free” over its citizens.

Here Commonalty names society’s organized common power: communes, workers’ councils, associated production, public services, shared knowledge, and the relationships that let people direct them.

It is a social relation, not the name of a ministry. An office cannot declare itself the Commonalty while people remain passive beneath it.

Three movements begin together

flowchart TD
    A["Working people build organs of direct power"] --> B["Useful public functions enter those organs"]
    B --> C["Economic and social transformation weakens class antagonism"]
    C --> D["Separate coercive power loses work and authority"]
    D --> E["The Commonalty becomes more universal"]
    E --> A

Text alternative: Self-government takes up useful public functions. Economic and social transformation weakens class conflict. Separate coercive power then loses its purpose. As the Commonalty expands, working people gain more capacity for self-government, and the cycle continues.

These movements depend on one another.

Workers’ power makes expropriation possible. Common provision gives people time to participate. Participation spreads administrative knowledge. Wider knowledge makes more reabsorption possible. Every successful transfer leaves less space for a caste of rulers.

The process can leap in one field and crawl in another. A neighborhood kitchen may become directly communal before a national electricity network. Unevenness does not change the direction.

Sublation means abolishing, preserving, and transforming in one movement. The Commonalty sublates the state.

It abolishes the state as a separate instrument of class domination. It preserves necessary capacities such as health records, laboratories, rail coordination, weather monitoring, emergency reserves, and conflict resolution. It transforms those capacities into transparent, accountable social activity.

This is why breaking the state cannot mean destroying every public system. A revolution that burns the medical archive and loses the insulin supply has not liberated medicine. A revolution that keeps the archive under a new secret hierarchy has not liberated it either.

The knowledge remains. The relation changes.

Free association is material. The universality of the Commonalty is reached when common life is generally organized through free association rather than class ownership, wage dependence, or a coercive power separated from society.

Free association means more than permission to leave. A worker who can reject an abusive employer only by losing food and shelter is formally free and materially trapped. People associate freely when necessities, knowledge, time, and social power are common enough for refusal and participation to be real.

Free association still produces obligations. A railway safety rule binds every passenger and worker. A drought plan may ration water. A community may restrain someone who is harming others. The difference is that these arrangements arise from common needs and remain under common control. They are not the command of an owning class.

New disputes will continue. History does not end. What ends is the material power of one class to freeze society around its privilege.

A state may own every factory while leaving people passive, knowledge locked away, officials secure, and coercive power untouched. A modest communal service that people understand, direct, correct, and gradually make less coercive may contain more of the new society.

The Commonalty is the transition seen from the side of what is being born. The state withers from the side of what is dying.

They are the same historical movement.

The state is sublated by the Commonalty until community as free association becomes universal.

Source notes

Primary texts

  • Friedrich Engels, letter to August Bebel, March 18, 1875, proposing Gemeinwesen in place of the phrase “free state.”
  • Karl Marx, The Civil War in France: First Draft, 1871, on the reabsorption of state power by society.
  • Karl Marx, Conspectus of Bakunin’s Statism and Anarchy, 1874, on the self-government of the commune.
  • Friedrich Engels, Anti-Dühring, Part III, chapter 2, “Theoretical”, 1877, on society taking possession of production, the administration of things, and the state’s eventual dying out.

Further study

  • Jacques Camatte, “Origin and Function of the Party Form”, 1961, on Gemeinwesen, alienation from human community, and the communist movement.
  • Peter Hudis, Marx’s Concept of the Alternative to Capitalism, 2012, on associated labor, the Commune, and the transcendence of alienated social forms.
