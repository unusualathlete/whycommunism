A Constitution for Communist Organization

A constitution does not tell an organization what to do at 9:00 tomorrow morning. It establishes what the organization is, what it exists to accomplish, and what it must not become while circumstances change.

The articles below are not sacred phrases. Each condenses a historical lesson bought through struggle, defeat, argument, and revolution.

Preamble

The communist movement organizes the working class to abolish capitalism, class rule, production for exchange, and every separate power that stands above society. Its end is a world community of freely associated producers who consciously govern common life.

Every structure, strategy, and tactic is judged from that direction.

Article I: Emancipation belongs to the class

Working-class emancipation must be the act of the working class itself.

The party concentrates programme, memory, education, preparation, and strategy because these capacities develop unevenly. It cannot turn that unevenness into a permanent right to govern. Councils, communes, unions, militias, and other class organs remain independently alive.

The test is severe: does political leadership enlarge the class’s capacity to understand and govern, or make the class dependent on the leader?

Article II: The final aim governs the road

Communism is not permanent state ownership, a fairer wage system, or national development with red symbols.

It abolishes classes, wage labour, production for exchange, and the opposition between public power and social life. Proletarian self-government is the transitional class power capable of carrying out that transformation. It is not a new social order to preserve forever.

An immediate gain can be worth defending without being mistaken for the final aim.

Article III: Materialism begins from real life

Communist organization studies people in their actual relations to work, property, nature, institutions, history, and one another.

It rejects two comforts. The first says that objective conditions decide everything, so organized action can only wait. The second says that sufficient will can abolish a condition that has not been understood.

Materialism investigates what exists, acts on it, and lets consequences correct thought. It needs active contact with scientific inquiry. A movement defending a theory from evidence has ceased to be materialist.

Article IV: The class keeps its independence

Class independence means that workers organize their own power and purpose rather than becoming the left wing of a capitalist faction, state, or national project.

Limited cooperation does not automatically destroy independence. Subordination does.

The boundary is crossed when money, access, protection, or office buys silence; when workers are asked to sacrifice for “the economy” while property remains safe; or when an alliance prevents the class from speaking and acting in its own name.

Article V: The struggle is international

Capital is a world relation. Production, finance, migration, war, and ecological breakdown cross borders.

Communists organize on the terrain where struggle actually occurs, including national and local terrains. They refuse to make one nation’s success the final measure. Internationalism demands material cooperation, shared strategy, refuge, mutual defense, and opposition to social chauvinism.

Coordination cannot mean rule by the strongest national organization. Voluntary world unity requires the ability of each field to report its real conditions and contest a line that sacrifices the movement to a dominant center.

Article VI: Centralism coordinates communist activity

Centralism is the concentration and coordination required for different organs to act toward one programme and strategy.

It is not the mechanical concentration of every decision or power.

The Communist International’s 1921 organizational theses warned that formal centralization can become the concentration of power in a bureaucracy. That warning matters more than the label attached to a structure. A center must be strong enough to combine activity and flexible enough to learn.

The proper level of authority follows the task, relevant knowledge, shared consequences, and the need for common action.

Article VII: Unity is active, not silent

Communist unity is produced by shared principles, truthful communication, understood decisions, disciplined action, and review.

Disagreement does not disappear when it is forbidden. It returns as rumor, passivity, faction, or filtered information.

Members need freedom to state evidence and criticize leadership. Decisions accepted through the organization’s legitimate process may still require coordinated implementation. Freedom without responsibility can become permanent commentary. Discipline without freedom can hide decomposition.

Article VIII: Anti-sectarianism keeps the whole class in view

Anti-sectarianism refuses to make a small group’s identity, inherited quarrels, trade position, nation, race, gender, or local advantage the measure of the proletariat as a whole.

It does not mean tolerating bigotry or dissolving political differences. Bigotry divides the class and must be fought. A communist organization explains disagreement through its material and political consequences, cooperates where class action is possible, and remains clear about what it cannot accept.

The party must be embedded in class life without reducing politics to whatever one section of workers presently believes.

Article IX: Forms serve functions

No committee, voting method, chain of command, territorial branch, workplace cell, or clandestine form is a principle by itself.

An organizational form is justified by the communist function it can perform under definite conditions. A temporary technical or military structure must not become a model for all social relations. An office built for one phase should disappear when its function ends.

Form changes. Direction remains.

The tensions belong inside the constitution

Principles do not erase conflict. They tell us how to recognize it.

These are not flaws added from outside. They arise because every useful capacity can detach from its purpose and become a power over the people who created it.

Communist principles keep returning organization to its reason for existing.

Not the glory of the party.

Not the survival of the apparatus.

The self-emancipation of the class and the end of class society.

Source notes

Primary texts

  • Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels, Manifesto of the Communist Party, section II, 1848. It defines the communists’ relation to the whole class, the conquest of political power, and the abolition of class distinctions.
  • Karl Marx, General Rules of the International Working Men’s Association, revised 1871. It joins self-emancipation, international union, local independence, and common coordination.
  • Vladimir Lenin, “On the Significance of Militant Materialism”, 1922. It argues for active materialist work in alliance with scientific inquiry rather than a closed political catechism.
  • Communist Party of Italy, Theses of Rome, 1922. It distinguishes programme, organization, and tactical action while confronting the dangers of improvisation and opportunism.

Further study

  • Onorato Damen, “Axioms of Revolutionary Theory and Practice”, 1974, on class independence, the party-class relation, and the political meaning of revolutionary organization.
  • Anton Pannekoek, “The Party and Class”, published 1941, on workers’ independent thought and action as the substance of emancipation.
