10Communist Production and Life

Alienation: When Our Own Powers Rule Us

People build workplaces, markets, machines, laws, and institutions. Then these creations confront people as if they were natural forces no one can control.

Marx called this alienation. Our own activity takes a form that becomes strange, separate, and powerful over us.

Alienation at work

A worker may make something useful but have no say over:

  • what is produced;
  • how it is produced;
  • who receives it;
  • what happens to the profit;
  • how fast the work is done; or
  • whether the workplace stays open.

The product belongs to someone else. The workday belongs to someone else. The worker’s cooperation with others appears as the organization of the employer.

The more power the worker creates for the firm, the more powerless the worker may feel.

Alienation is bigger than a feeling

Alienation can feel like boredom, anxiety, or distance, but it is not only a mood. It is built into social relations.

A cheerful worker can still lack control over the product and process. A miserable worker may understand their social power very clearly.

Changing attitude alone cannot end alienation. The conditions of activity must change.

Objectification is not always alienation

Objectification means putting an ability or idea into an object or result. A carpenter objectifies skill in a table. A writer objectifies thought in a story.

This can be fulfilling. People recognize themselves in what they create.

Alienation appears when the object and the social process behind it escape the producer’s control and return as an independent power. The table becomes mainly a commodity. The story becomes intellectual property controlled by a company. The machine becomes a way to monitor and discipline its operator.

Money gathers social power

Money gives access to other people’s labour and to the products of society. This makes it a concentrated social power.

Abilities a person does not have can appear to belong to them because they can buy the result. Powers a person truly has may count for little when they cannot sell them.

The problem is not that exchange is rude. It is that human relations pass through a thing that measures and limits them.

Communism aims to return that social power to direct human association.

Species-being

Marx used species-being for the distinctly human power to develop life consciously and socially. Human beings make tools, languages, art, institutions, and plans. They transform nature and themselves together.

This power is not a fixed list of traits. Human nature develops through history.

The question is what kinds of people a mode of production helps create. A society organized around competition trains different senses, desires, habits, and knowledge than one organized around common provision and free cooperation.

Human development as wealth

If wealth means money, a society appears richer when more things are sold. If wealth means developed human powers, the measure changes.

Free time, health, knowledge, emotional capacity, creative skill, trustworthy relationships, and access to nature become central forms of wealth.

Communist production aims to make the development of each person part of the purpose of production itself.

Alienation is not every form of difficulty

Ending alienation does not mean every activity feels joyful. Frustration, grief, conflict, and difficult effort belong to human life.

It also does not mean people dissolve into one collective mind. Alienation ends through richer relations among distinct people, not through the destruction of individuality.

No government can declare a “new human” into existence. Human capacities change through new material and social activity over time.

A live argument, not a footnote

Alienation is bound to private property, wage labour, production for exchange, money, and the division of labour.

Marx’s humanism and the meaning of species-being remain debated. The companion Study will compare the interpretations, identify their textual grounds, and explain what each one reveals or obscures.
